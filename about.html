About

Accounting researcher, curious observer.

I am a Ph.D. student in Accountancy at The Chinese University of Hong Kong. Before joining CUHK, I completed both my M.Phil. and B.B.A. in Accounting at Shanghai University of Finance and Economics.

Biography

My research interests include the real effects of disclosure, information intermediaries and processing costs, and the welfare consequences of regulation. I am interested in how information changes decisions—and in the institutions that determine who can process and act on it.

My earlier experience in fixed-income research continues to inform my interest in how market participants interpret complex disclosures and regulatory change.
